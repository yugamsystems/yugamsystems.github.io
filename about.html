Background

I started on a factory floor at Vedanta Aluminium in Jharsuguda, Odisha — managing production shifts, leading a team of 15, and reducing scrap generation by 44% through process re-engineering. From there, nearly three years at KPMG India running operational risk and process improvement engagements for Fortune 500 clients across manufacturing, cement, and natural resources. Specific results: logistics costs down INR 2.4 crore for one client, a procurement cycle shortened by 10 days for another, lead consultant on five consecutive projects at a single client.

After an MBA at ISB, I joined Facebook in Dublin in 2016, then moved to Singapore — spending a decade at Meta across Community Operations, Business Integrity, Sales Partnerships, and Customer Experience Strategy. I left in May 2026 to start Yugam. Understanding where AI will change something in a specific business — and where it won't — requires reading the operations, not just the tool catalogue.

How I think
On reading operations
You read a process by watching where it breaks. Not by reading a diagram of how it should work.
On AI adoption
Find the problem first. Then see whether AI is the right tool for it. The implementations that fail skipped that step.
On what makes transformation fail
A working AI system that the team doesn't use is a failed implementation. The technology is never the hard part.
On the first conversation
The most useful thing I can do in a first meeting is tell you honestly whether you need what I'm selling.
