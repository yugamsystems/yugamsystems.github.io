Background

I started on a factory floor at Vedanta Aluminium in Jharsuguda, Odisha — managing production shifts, coordinating maintenance schedules, and leading a team of 15 people to hit monthly output targets in a live smelting operation. Before the MBA, before the tech company, before AI was a business category: that was three years of learning that operations improvement is a hands-on discipline. You read a process by watching where it breaks, not by reading a diagram of how it should work. We reduced scrap generation by 44%. The method behind that result is the same method I apply today.

From Jharsuguda, I moved into management consulting at KPMG India — nearly three years running operational risk and process improvement engagements for Fortune 500 clients across manufacturing, consumer goods, cement, and natural resources. The work was specific: logistics costs down by INR 2.4 crore for one client, a procurement cycle shortened by 10 days for another. I was retained as lead consultant on five consecutive projects at a single client, which resulted in a contract renewal worth INR 90 lacs. The pattern I noticed across every engagement was consistent — organisations weren't measuring the things that mattered, and they didn't know where the process was leaking.

Organisations weren't measuring the things that mattered, and they didn't know where the process was leaking.

After an MBA in operations and finance at the Indian School of Business, I joined Facebook in Dublin in 2016. Over the next decade — moving to Singapore in 2018 — I worked across five different roles: Community Operations, Business Integrity, Sales Partnerships, and then Customer Experience Strategy and Operations, where I ended as Senior Program Manager. The scope changed with each role, but the core work didn't. I spent a decade at Meta figuring out why complex systems weren't working and building the processes to fix them, across dozens of markets and hundreds of millions of users.

I left Meta in May 2026 to start Yugam. The case for AI in mid-market business operations is clear — the tools are mature, the cost is manageable, and the ROI is measurable. What most businesses are missing is someone who understands operations well enough to know where AI will actually change something, and where it will add cost and complexity without a return. That's the specific problem Yugam is here to solve.
