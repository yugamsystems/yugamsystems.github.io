Foundation Assessment

Three things I check before recommending any AI tool.

Most businesses jump straight to tools. Which LLM? Which platform? Which vendor? But the technology is never the bottleneck. These three things are.

Data Organization
Is your data structured, documented, and accessible? Or scattered across spreadsheets, inboxes, and tribal knowledge?
Process Clarity
Are your standard operating procedures documented? Or does critical knowledge live in people's heads, vulnerable to turnover?
Team Readiness
Are your teams prepared to work alongside AI? Or will adoption stall because the change was imposed, not enabled?
"I've watched teams spend six months building an AI solution on top of data that no one trusted. The AI worked. The data and processes around it didn't. They had to start over."
If any of these three are fragile — data, process, or people — that's where I start. Every time. Without exception.

How an engagement runs

Three engagements. Every one outputs something tangible.

Three engagements — Assess, Build, Enable — expanded into six detailed steps on the Methodology page. Each phase ends with a deliverable your team owns.

Full methodology →
01
Assess
2 – 4 weeks

AI readiness audit across data, process, and people. Opportunity map ranked by value and feasibility. Risk flags that could derail a deployment. Output: a go/no-go recommendation with specifics — not a hedged summary.

02
Build
8 – 16 weeks

Strategy and use-case prioritisation. Tool selection and vendor evaluation with no vendor bias. Workflow architecture and pilot build. Iteration until it works in production, not just in demo conditions. Output: a working AI system with a measurement framework.

03
Enable
Final 6 – 8 weeks

Role-specific training and adoption support. Team readiness mapped and addressed before handover. A 90-day post-launch review built into the engagement. Output: your team running the system independently — no dependency on Yugam to keep it going.
