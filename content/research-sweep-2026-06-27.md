### 1. Deloitte: 66% report productivity gains, but only 34% deeply transform
**Source:** Deloitte | **Format:** Annual Report | **Relevance:** 5/5
**URL:** https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/what-we-do/capabilities/applied-artificial-intelligence/content/state-of-ai-in-the-enterprise.html
**Key finding:** Survey of 3,235 leaders across 24 countries. 66% of organizations report AI productivity gains. But only 34% say AI is "deeply transforming" their business. Worker access to AI rose 50% in 2025. 85% expect to customize AI agents. The skills gap — not the technology — remains the #1 barrier.
**Rohit's angle:** This is the readiness gap in numbers. Two-thirds get surface-level productivity gains (faster emails, quicker summaries) but only a third achieve real transformation. Why? Because productivity gains require no organizational change — transformation requires data, process, and people to be ready. The 34% who transform are the ones who did the foundation work first. This directly validates the Yugam methodology: start with readiness, not tools.
**Content opportunity:** NEWSLETTER + LINKEDIN
**Draft angle:** "66% of companies got faster at the things that don't matter. Only 34% changed how their business actually works. Here's what separates the two groups."

### 2. HBR: Why AI Adoption Stalls — Industry Data
**Source:** Harvard Business Review | **Relevance:** 5/5
**URL:** https://hbr.org/2026/02/why-ai-adoption-stalls-according-to-industry-data

People aren't resisting AI because they're Luddites — they're protecting their identity and relevance. This is a change management problem, not a technology problem. My decade at Meta managing trust & safety teams across 15+ countries taught me this: adoption stalls when people feel threatened, not when tools are hard to use. The Team Readiness Check addresses exactly this.

### 4. SMB Data: 76% use AI but only 14% embed it in operations

The confidence gap (27% vs 82%) between small and mid-sized firms is an opportunity — mid-market firms know enough to be ready but not enough to execute. That's exactly who Yugam serves.

## Patterns & Themes This Week

Three themes are converging across all sources this week:

1. **The adoption-impact gap is widening, not closing.** More companies are "using" AI (78% per McKinsey, 76% per SMB data) but fewer are capturing real value (19% McKinsey, 14% SMB, 34% Deloitte). The technology is easier to deploy; the organizational readiness required to make it work hasn't improved. This is the Yugam thesis validated by every major research body simultaneously.

2. **The human dimension is finally getting attention.** WEF, BCG, and HBR are all converging on the same insight: AI adoption is a workforce/people problem, not a technology problem. Employee anxiety, identity, and change fatigue are named explicitly as root causes of failure — not data quality or integration (those are symptoms).

3. **Governance is moving from "nice to have" to regulatory requirement.** OECD due diligence framework, Colorado AI Act, and California SB 53 are creating a compliance landscape that will force governance onto every AI deployment. Companies that built governance early (Rohit's KPMG lens) will have a structural advantage.
